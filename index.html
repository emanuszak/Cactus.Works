About Me

I'm an aspiring front-end developer and a recovering public relations professional.

I've spent more than eight years doing strategic communications and media relations in various industries—most recently I've helped startups and tech companies launch products and share their company's mission. I'm known for being able to understand the nuts and bolts of the tech companies I've worked with, from the back-end up through the consumer experience.

I taught myself how to code when I was in 6th grade, in 1996. After spending about four years talking about tech, I want to get back to that original passion and start actually building tech as a full-time web developer. I offer more than just technical ability to manipulate pixels; my experience in brand messaging gives me a uniquely holistic viewpoint on how to integrate content and code to meet a project's goals.

My personal interests include health and fitness—I run distances from the 5K to the marathon, stand-up paddleboard in the Hudson River, bike around Brooklyn and Manhattan, and I occasionally venture to Staten Island to golf. I also love making things from scratch, be it knitting or baking bread. My latest venture is homebrewing sake.

I'm available for hire for web development projects and select public relations work.
