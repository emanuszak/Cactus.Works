Who is Ellen?

I'm an aspiring front-end developer, a recovering public relations professional, and staunch supporter of the Oxford comma.

I've spent more than eight years doing strategic communications and media relations in various industries—most recently I've launched startups and helped tech companies share their stories. I'm known for being able to understand the nuts and bolts of the tech companies I've worked with, from the back-end up through the consumer experience.

I taught myself how to code when I was in 6th grade, in 1996. After spending about four years talking about tech, I want to get back to that original passion and start actually building tech as a full-time web developer. I offer more than just technical ability to manipulate pixels; my background in PR gives me a unique perspective on how code and front-end can work with content to effectively deliver a brand's message.

My personal interests include health and fitness—I run distances from the 5K to the marathon, stand-up paddleboard in the Hudson River, bike around Brooklyn and Manhattan, and I occasionally venture to Staten Island to golf. I also love making things from scratch, be it knitting or baking bread. My latest venture is homebrewing sake.

I'm available for hire for web development projects, and select public relations work.
